Tips

  1. Strong Passwords: Use passwords with 12 to 16 characters, including letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid personal information and repeating characters.
  2. Malware Prevention: Regularly update your software to protect against malicious software (malware) that can harm your devices.
  3. Avoid Phishing Attacks: Be cautious of scam emails pretending to be trustworthy sources. Verify before clicking on any suspicious links.
  4. Backup Your Data: Regularly back up your important files to prevent data loss.
  5. Device Safety: Lock your devices with a PIN or password, and never leave them unprotected in public.

Tricks

  1. Windows Key + L: Press these keys together to lock your computer instantly. It requires a password to log back in.
  2. Screensaver Password: Set up a password-protected screensaver. It locks your keyboard when you step away from the computer.
